## ACTION BY UNANIMOUS WRITTEN CONSENT OF THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS OF [COMPANY NAME], INC.

The undersigned, constituting all of the members of the Board of Directors (the "**Board**") of [COMPANY NAME], Inc., a Delaware corporation (the "**Company**"), acting pursuant to Section 141(f) of the Delaware General Corporation Law and the Company's Bylaws, hereby adopt the following resolutions by unanimous written consent without a meeting, effective as of [DATE]:

### 1. ESTABLISHMENT OF SECONDARY LIQUIDITY PROGRAM

**WHEREAS**, the Board acknowledges that due to extended private market timelines, Company stockholders (including current and former service providers and early-stage investors) may desire liquidity prior to a Company exit event;

**WHEREAS**, the Board determines it is in the best interests of the Company to standardize the administration of such secondary transfers to ensure compliance with securities laws, protect the Company's 409A valuation, maintain cap table hygiene, and minimize administrative burden;

**WHEREAS**, the Board has reviewed the Simple Agreement for Liquidity Exit (the "**SALE**"), an open-source standardized instrument for secondary stock transfers available at simpleagreementforliquidity.com; and

**WHEREAS**, the Board desires to adopt a policy to pre-approve certain secondary transfers conducted using the SALE, subject to the conditions set forth below.

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, that the Secondary Liquidity Program (the "**Program**") is hereby established, subject to the Program Guidelines attached hereto as Exhibit A.

### 2. APPROVAL OF FORM DOCUMENTS

**RESOLVED**, that the form of Simple Agreement for Liquidity Exit (SALE), substantially in the form attached hereto as Exhibit B (the "**Form SALE**"), is hereby approved for use in connection with transfers authorized under the Program.

### 3. DELEGATION OF AUTHORITY

**RESOLVED**, that the Company's [Chief Executive Officer] and [Chief Financial Officer] (each, an "**Authorized Officer**") are hereby authorized and empowered, in their sole discretion, to administer the Program, including reviewing proposed SALEs, confirming compliance with the Program Guidelines, executing the Form SALE on behalf of the Company, and processing transfers.

### 4. WAIVER OF ROFR

**RESOLVED**, that subject to a transfer's strict compliance with the Program Guidelines, the Board hereby waives any Right of First Refusal (ROFR) held by the Company pursuant to its Bylaws, Certificate of Incorporation, or any Company Agreement (as defined in the Form SALE) with respect to such transfer; provided, however, that the Authorized Officers shall ensure that any corresponding rights held by Major Investors (as defined in the Company's Right of First Refusal and Co-Sale Agreement) have been waived or have lapsed prior to the closing of any such transfer.

### 5. ADMINISTRATIVE FEE

**RESOLVED**, that the Authorized Officers are empowered to collect the administrative fee set forth in the Form SALE in connection with each transfer under the Program, and that such fees shall be applied to offset the Company's legal and administrative costs of processing secondary transfers.

### 6. REPORTING

**RESOLVED**, that the Authorized Officers shall provide a summary of all SALE transactions to the Board on a [quarterly / annual] basis, including for each transaction: (i) the names of the Seller and Buyer, (ii) the number and class of shares transferred, (iii) the purchase price per share and aggregate purchase price, (iv) the discount applied, and (v) confirmation that all Program Guidelines were satisfied.

### 7. TRANSACTIONS REQUIRING SEPARATE BOARD APPROVAL

**RESOLVED**, that any proposed secondary transfer that does not satisfy all of the conditions set forth in the Program Guidelines (as set forth on Exhibit A), including any transaction that exceeds the per-transaction or volume limits, any transfer to a non-eligible Buyer, any transfer during a blackout period, or any transfer using a modified form of SALE, shall require separate approval by the Board on a case-by-case basis.

### 8. AMENDMENT AND REVOCATION

**RESOLVED**, that this Program and the Program Guidelines may be amended, modified, suspended, or revoked at any time by resolution of the Board.

**RESOLVED FURTHER**, that the officers of the Company are hereby authorized and directed to take any and all actions and to execute and deliver any and all documents and instruments as may be necessary or desirable to carry out the intent and purposes of the foregoing resolutions.

## EXHIBIT A: PROGRAM GUIDELINES

The Authorized Officers may approve secondary transfers and execute a SALE on behalf of the Company only if ALL of the following conditions are met.

### 1. Form Integrity

The transaction uses the standard SALE form (v1.0 or any successor version approved by the Board) without modification, except for the completion of blanks and bracketed terms as contemplated by the form.

### 2. Per-Transaction Size Limit

The aggregate purchase price of the Transferred Shares in any single SALE does not exceed $[_______] [e.g., $250,000].

### 3. Volume Cap

The aggregate number of shares transferred under this Program in any rolling 12-month period shall not exceed [___]% [e.g., 5%] of the Company's fully diluted capitalization as of the beginning of such period.

### 4. Pricing Guardrails (409A Protection)

To prevent an adverse impact on the Company's 409A valuation:

(a) **Ceiling:** The purchase price per share shall not exceed the price per share of the Company's most recent Preferred Stock financing (to avoid an "up-round" signal that could increase the 409A FMV and raise the strike price of future option grants).

(b) **Floor:** The discount to the Reference Price shall not exceed [___]% [e.g., 30%] (to avoid a "distressed sale" signal that could depress the 409A FMV or trigger auditor scrutiny).

### 5. Eligible Buyers

Transfers may only be made to Buyers who satisfy ALL of the following:

(a) The Buyer is an "Accredited Investor" as defined in Rule 501(a) of Regulation D under the Securities Act of 1933, as amended.

(b) The Buyer is either: (i) an existing Major Investor (as defined in the Company Agreements); or (ii) an institutional buyer or other person approved by an Authorized Officer, who agrees to be bound by the Market Stand-Off (Lock-Up) provision in the Investors' Rights Agreement.

(c) The Buyer is not a competitor of the Company, an employee or agent of a competitor, or any other person whose ownership of Company stock would, in the reasonable judgment of the Authorized Officer, be adverse to the interests of the Company.

(d) Neither the Buyer nor any of its control persons is subject to "Bad Actor" disqualification under Rule 506(d) of the Securities Act.

### 6. Seller Eligibility

(a) Only stockholders who have held their shares for at least [___] months [e.g., 12] are eligible to sell under the Program (aligning with Rule 144 holding period requirements).

(b) Neither the Seller nor any of its control persons is subject to "Bad Actor" disqualification under Rule 506(d) of the Securities Act.

### 7. ROFR Compliance

The Authorized Officer has confirmed that the Company has provided notice of the proposed transfer to any Major Investors holding rights of first refusal with respect to the Transferred Shares under the Company's Right of First Refusal and Co-Sale Agreement, and that either (i) such Major Investors have waived their rights in writing, or (ii) the applicable notice and exercise periods have lapsed without exercise.

### 8. Blackout Periods

No SALE transaction may close during any of the following periods:

(a) The period between a fiscal quarter-end and the date the Company's financial statements for such quarter are finalized (the "Quiet Period").

(b) Any period during which the Company is engaged in active negotiations regarding a merger, acquisition, or other change-of-control transaction, as determined by an Authorized Officer.

(c) Any other blackout period designated by the Board or an Authorized Officer by written notice to the Company's stockholders.

### 9. Administrative Fee

The applicable party (Seller or Buyer, as designated in the SALE) has agreed to pay the administrative fee specified in the Form SALE to the Company at Closing.

### 10. Standard Disclosure Packet

The Authorized Officer has arranged for delivery to the Buyer of the Standard Disclosure Packet as described in Section 3(c) of the Form SALE, consisting of: (i) the Company's most recent unaudited balance sheet and income statement, (ii) a summary capitalization table (showing the Buyer's post-transaction percentage ownership on a fully diluted basis), and (iii) a certification of good standing.
